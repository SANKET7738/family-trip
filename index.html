Day-wise itinerary
1
Arrival in Kathmandu
Flight from India · hotel check-in · rest
▾
Fly from your city to Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu
Check in to hotel — typically 3-star in Thamel area
Welcome dinner with the tour group
Evening stroll around Thamel market (optional)
2
Kathmandu city tour
Pashupatinath · Boudhanath · Swayambhunath
▾
Pashupatinath Temple — one of the holiest Hindu shrines in the world, on the banks of the Bagmati river
Boudhanath Stupa — massive Buddhist stupa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple) — hilltop temple with panoramic city views
Kathmandu Durbar Square — ancient royal palace and courtyards
3
Nagarkot + Patan
Himalayan sunrise views · UNESCO heritage
▾
Early morning drive to Nagarkot — famous for sunrise views of the Himalayas including Everest range on clear days
Return to Kathmandu, visit Patan Durbar Square (UNESCO site)
Patan Museum — one of the best museums in Asia showcasing Newari crafts
Evening at leisure — souvenir shopping in Thamel
4
Drive to Pokhara
Scenic 6–7 hr drive along Prithvi Highway
▾
Morning drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara — scenic road through mountains and river gorges
Arrival at Pokhara — check in to lakeside hotel
Evening on Phewa Lake — boat ride with stunning Annapurna range backdrop
Lakeside promenade for dinner and local food exploration
5
Pokhara sightseeing
Sarangkot sunrise · caves · World Peace Stupa
▾
Early morning drive to Sarangkot viewpoint — breathtaking sunrise over Annapurna and Fishtail peaks
Devi's Falls (Patale Chhango) — a waterfall that disappears into an underground tunnel
Gupteshwor Mahadev Cave — sacred cave temple right next to the falls
World Peace Stupa — hilltop white stupa with 360° views of the Himalayas and the lake
6
Chitwan National Park
Jungle safari · elephant experience · rhinos
▾
Drive or fly to Chitwan — UNESCO World Heritage jungle reserve
Jeep safari in the national park — one-horned rhinos, crocodiles, deer and if lucky, tigers
Elephant breeding centre visit — see and interact with baby elephants
Tharu cultural dance show in the evening at the jungle resort
7
Chitwan activities + return
Canoe ride · bird watching · drive back
▾
Morning canoe ride on Rapti River — spot gharials and marsh mugger crocodiles
Nature walk with a naturalist guide — birdwatching (Chitwan has 500+ bird species)
Drive back to Kathmandu
Farewell dinner and last shopping
8
Departure
Transfer to Kathmandu airport · fly home
▾
Morning transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport
Last-minute souvenirs at the airport — pashmina, thangka art, teas
Fly back to India

Why Nepal?

Cheapest option by far. Zero visa hassle for Indians. Chitwan is brilliant for kids — jungle jeep safaris, elephant encounters, and crocodiles. Pokhara is one of the most scenic lake cities in Asia.

Watch out

Monsoon starts late June — mountain views get cloudy. Best to travel in May if possible. Stick to cities and Chitwan for June travel rather than trekking areas.

Day-wise itinerary
1
Arrive Paro
One of the world's most scenic airports
▾
Fly into Paro International Airport — landing through mountain valleys is spectacular
Drive to Thimphu (1 hour) — check in to hotel
Evening stroll at Thimphu market and Clock Tower Square
2
Thimphu city tour
Capital city · Buddha statue · Royal Takin Preserve
▾
Buddha Dordenma — massive gilded Buddha statue with panoramic views of the valley (51 meters tall)
Royal Takin Preserve — see the Takin, Bhutan's national animal (a bizarre goat-antelope hybrid)
Tashichho Dzong — fortress-monastery that houses the throne room and offices of the King
National Handicraft Center and Folk Heritage Museum
3
Thimphu → Punakha
Dochula Pass · Punakha Dzong
▾
Drive over Dochula Pass (3,100m) — 108 memorial chortens with stunning Himalayan views on clear days
Punakha Dzong — the most beautiful dzong in Bhutan, at the confluence of two rivers
Suspension bridge walk over the Mo Chhu river
Check in to Punakha resort
4
Punakha → Phobjika Valley
Black-necked crane sanctuary · glacial valley
▾
Drive to Phobjika Valley — a glacial valley that is winter home to rare black-necked cranes
Gangtey Monastery — one of the most important Nyingma monasteries in Bhutan
Black-Necked Crane Information Center
Scenic walks through the valley farmland
5–6
Paro valley + excursions
Rinpung Dzong · traditional farmhouse visit
▾
Drive to Paro — check in to hotel
Rinpung Dzong (Paro Dzong) — fortress overlooking the Paro valley
National Museum of Bhutan — housed in a watchtower
Visit to a traditional Bhutanese farmhouse and interact with locals
Chele La Pass drive — highest motorable road in Bhutan at 3,988m
7
Tiger's Nest Hike
Paro Taktsang — the icon of Bhutan
▾
The highlight of the trip — hike up to Tiger's Nest Monastery (Paro Taktsang), clinging to a cliffside at 3,120m
About 4–5 hour round trip hike — mules available for those who need assistance
The monastery dates to 1692 and is one of the most sacred sites in Bhutan
Packed lunch on the mountain — views are extraordinary
8–9
Leisure + Departure
Shopping · hot stone bath · fly home
▾
Traditional Bhutanese hot stone bath (dotsho) — a rejuvenating ritual
Shopping for handwoven textiles, wooden masks, incense, and Bhutanese crafts
Farewell dinner with local Bhutanese cuisine (ema datshi — chili cheese stew)
Transfer to Paro Airport and fly home

Why Bhutan? 🏆

The happiest country on earth. Zero pollution, no traffic signals, pristine mountains. Tiger's Nest is one of the most unforgettable family experiences anywhere. May–June has the best weather of all destinations. Indian Rupees accepted everywhere — no exchange needed.

Good to know

Since 2023, Indians no longer need the Sustainable Development Fee (SDF) permit — a significant saving. Tours sometimes depart from Kolkata or Bagdogra, so check your departure city. Indian mobile SIMs work in Bhutan (BSNL roaming).

Weather warning

🌞 Perfect timing — May–June is Bali's DRY SEASON

While Thailand, Vietnam and Sri Lanka deal with monsoon in May–June, Bali enjoys its best weather: clear skies, low humidity, perfect beach days. This alone makes Bali the standout pick for your travel window.

Why Bali? 🏆

May–June is dry season — the best weather while everyone else deals with rain. 7 full nights. Safari Park for the kids, Nusa Penida for the adults, cultural temples, incredible food, and some of the best sunsets on earth. Visa is just $35 cash at the airport.

Visa tip

Carry USD 35 per person in cash specifically for the Visa on Arrival counter. Exchange your USD in India before flying — rates are much better. The VOA queue at Ngurah Rai Airport usually moves in 10–15 minutes.

Why it's over budget

Package alone is ₹76–82k. Add internal flight (~₹5k), e-visa (~₹2.1k), GST, insurance — total crosses ₹1.2 lakh per person. If budget is flexible, Vietnam is extraordinary. If not, Bali gives you more value for the same window.

Weather is a real concern

North Vietnam (Hanoi, Ha Long Bay) gets heavy monsoon rains in June. The overnight cruise — the trip highlight — can be severely affected by rough seas and poor visibility. If you do Vietnam, ideally go October–March.

E-visa tip

Apply at evisa.gov.vn — official government portal, $25 for single entry (90-day validity). Apply at least 7–10 days before travel. Download and print the approved e-visa to show at immigration.

What makes Singapore special

The single best destination for kids on this entire list. Universal Studios, Night Safari, Sentosa, Gardens by the Bay — all world-class. Consistently safe, English-speaking, immaculately clean, and well-organised. Weather is good year-round.

Why it's so expensive

Singapore is one of the world's most expensive cities. Only breakfast is covered — a family lunch easily costs ₹1,500–2,500 and dinner ₹2,000–4,000. Add flights (if not included — verify!), visa (₹3,100/person), Grab rides, and Orchard Road shopping. The numbers stack up fast.
